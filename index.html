That gap is precisely what Era is engineered to close.

A Practitioner's Reality: The Anjali Scenario

Practitioner Case Study

The Senior Analyst Who Manages Billions - But Not Her Own Portfolio

Anjali is a senior equity research analyst at a mid-size asset management firm with approximately $2.8 billion in assets under management. She covers the healthcare and pharmaceutical sectors, contributes to weekly investment committee deliberations, and routinely advises institutional clients on forward P/E adjustments, sector rotation rationale, and earnings deviation analysis.

She has already integrated ChatGPT for first-draft commentary and uses Claude to interrogate lengthy regulatory filings for material risk disclosures. These integrations have reclaimed roughly eight to ten hours per week - bandwidth now redirected toward higher-conviction research and client-facing advisory work.

The structural irony is not lost on her colleagues: Anjali advises institutional counterparts on portfolio construction and capital allocation strategy with considerable authority. Her own retirement portfolio has remained in its original default allocation since 2019. Her emergency reserve sits in a low-yield savings account earning materially below prevailing money market rates. She has not rebalanced following two consecutive quarters of material equity drawdown.

Era was introduced into her workflow as a contextual data layer that her existing AI assistants could finally interrogate in real time. Within a single session, Claude - connected to Era's Context MCP server - surfaced her complete account picture, flagged the yield differential on her savings allocation, identified three materially elevated recurring charges, and modeled a preliminary rebalancing scenario against her stated risk tolerance. No manual data entry. No platform context-switching. No CSV export cycle. That is the value proposition Era articulates.

Technical Context - What Is MCP?

Model Context Protocol (MCP) is an open standard, analogous to a universal API layer for AI agent integrations. Rather than requiring each application to build bespoke connections to each AI platform, MCP establishes a standardized protocol through which any compliant assistant can securely query and interact with connected data sources. Era's Context product functions as an MCP server for your financial life. If your preferred AI assistant adds MCP support in the future, Era's integration carries over immediately - no reconfiguration required.

Establishing the Context MCP Connection

Within Claude, navigate to Settings - Integrations - Add MCP server, and register the Context endpoint: context.era.app. The same process applies within ChatGPT and other MCP-compatible clients. You retain granular control over read versus write permissions - restricting your agent to analytical queries only, or extending it to execute transfers and configure automated rules, entirely at your discretion.

4

Interrogate your financial position in natural language

With Context active, your AI assistant responds to queries against your real financial data. "What is my current liquidity position across all accounts?" "Which recurring charges have increased materially over the past six months?" "Model a scenario in which I allocate an additional $500 per month to my highest-yield savings vehicle." These are grounded, personalized analyses drawn from your live account data.

Cross-Platform Memory: A Structural Advantage

Among Era's less prominently discussed architectural features is its implementation of persistent, cross-platform financial memory. Under the current paradigm, conversational context within any AI assistant is session-bound. A directive communicated to Claude - "I am targeting a down payment reserve of $60,000 by Q3 2026, prioritized against all discretionary reallocation decisions" - exists only within that conversation window. Era's Context architecture stores goals, behavioral rules, account parameters, and stated preferences in Era's data layer rather than within any individual AI assistant's session memory.

Security, Regulatory Standing, and Data Governance

Encryption Standards

Era employs bank-grade encryption for all financial data, both in transit and at rest. All data communications are secured to institutional cryptographic standards equivalent to those maintained by regulated depository institutions.

Credential Handling

Era connects to financial institutions exclusively through Plaid and regulated aggregation partners. Your banking credentials are never transmitted to or stored within Era's infrastructure. Plaid functions as the credentialed intermediary under its own regulatory framework.

Data Monetization Policy

Era operates on a subscription revenue model. Your personal financial data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or leveraged for third-party monetization. The platform's commercial incentives are structurally aligned with user retention - not data exploitation.

AI Model Training

Your financial data is never used to train AI models - neither Era's own systems nor the third-party AI assistants with which Context integrates. Data access by connected AI assistants is permissioned, auditable, and governed entirely by the access controls you configure.

Regulatory Standing

Era is registered with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission as a Registered Investment Adviser (RIA) under Era Financial Advisers LLC. This registration confers a legally enforceable fiduciary duty and subjects the platform to ongoing SEC examination and oversight.

Recommended Onboarding

For professionals new to AI-enabled financial automation, initiate in read-only mode. Establish confidence in the platform's analytical output before enabling transactional permissions. Grant write access incrementally, as the system's behavior is validated through direct observation.

The Allocation's Assessment

Era represents a structurally distinct development in the fintech landscape - not because it introduces a novel personal finance paradigm, but because it provides the connective tissue that converts general-purpose AI assistants into financially contextualized analytical counterparts. It can maintain full awareness of your account positions, stated objectives, behavioral constraints, and historical patterns - persistently, across platforms, and in real time.
